# Destry R. Saul, Ph.D.


destry@hamdensaul.solutions  
[github.com/destrys](https://github.com/destrys)

>  Problems don't scare astronomers, there isn't anything they won't work on.
>  But the best thing about them is that they can tell you in ten minutes
>  if a project isn't possible.

This quote sums up what I bring to table: no problem is too complex,
no data are too dirty, but if there is something fundamentally wrong
with a project, I will spot it. I love working on projects that haven't
been done before and where Googling won't find you an answer.
That is what attracted me to the bitcoin and blockchain space for my
last project. Whether I'm developing software, digging into data,
or writing a spec, I approach my work with pragmatism, and with an eye
to deliver increasing value.

While I love a tough problem to bang my head against,
I also know taking a project to production requires thorough testing
and a critical eye.
In my last role,
I developed protocols for safely storing and transferring
hundreds of millions of dollars worth of digital assets.
There is no room for breaking things in that environment.

Products are not built and deployed in sealed bubbles, they are built
by people and for people. It is important to me that both my teams and
my products are intentionally inclusive. Besides being self-critical, I
welcome feedback of how I can be more sensitive of my colleauges,
stakeholders, and customers. I believe that companies that share
this value will be more successful and do more good than companies
that do not.
